Microtarget

  • Design
  • Illustration
  • Copywriting

The Microtarget unit was first conceived as a means to get more Meetup groups started by users who may not have thought to start one otherwise. I worked with a product manager to coordinate with the backend engineers and target users based on criteria like their location, if there were local Meetups relevant to a user's interests, and how long they'd been using Meetup. This information was then used to pick a unit a populate it with content relevant to that user. The Microtarget experiements helped us learn a little more about what makes people less anxious about starting a Meetup group. At it's peak, the Microtarget helped increase traffic into the group start funnel 45%

This unit was even used to target other helpful messaging to users, like letting organizers know they were close to reaching the 50-member limit on the Basic organizer subscription, and asking members to chip in money to their organizers after they had RSVP'd yes to events in their group.

It needed to be smart under the hood, but simple enough that a product manager or designer wouldn't have to ask engineers to update the content or set up the rules for who to target targeting every time we wanted to create a new Microtarget.

This unit was rapidly iterated and experimented on. We had to build small, sharp tools to make managing and monitoring this unit simpler for stakeholders.

One of the admin tools was simply for measuring the success of the units and toggling them on or off

We also needed a tool for creating multi-variant tests with different copy and image treatments when applicable

Another admin tool was used as an interface for the backend creating criteria a user must fulfill to see this unit and where they would see it
